🌍 World Summary
Vision Statement
In 2035, mental health is a collective priority thanks to empathetic AI networks and neuro-sensory interfaces that foster continuous, personalized emotional healing. Emotions are no longer hidden—they are understood, shared, and transformed into collective strength.

🔬 Technology
Empathetic Neuro-AI in emotional healing ecosystems
Empathetic Neuro-AI detects emotional states using neural signals and biosensors, offering real-time therapeutic interventions. Daily life has changed: everyone is supported by “Emotional Coaches”—AI companions that prevent emotional crises and promote healing before distress escalates.

What kind of AI exists in your world?
Empathetic, context-aware tool AI. Each Emotional Coach is co-trained by the user and certified human therapists. Developed by ethical AI collectives and neuroscience labs, the AI evolves with the individual. It doesn’t prescribe; it listens, adapts, and co-navigates emotional complexity alongside its human partner.

New or Reformed Institution
RaízMental Global:

An intercontinental nonprofit that regulates, funds, and supervises AI-powered emotional care ecosystems. RaízMental merges neuroethics, emotional justice, and AI design to ensure that empathetic AI serves liberation, not control. It certifies hybrid therapists (human + AI), enforces algorithmic transparency, and promotes the “Democracy of Feeling,” where emotions shape public life and policy.

Transformed Sector
Mental and Emotional Healthcare
By 2035, mental health will be integrated into everyday life. It’s no longer pathologized or hidden behind clinical walls. Cities have emotional resonance hubs—public spaces where people can decompress, engage in digital rituals, or access micro-therapies. Emotional well-being is viewed as a form of infrastructure, much like water or power. Suicide rates have dropped, empathy has risen, and emotional literacy is central to education and leadership.

A Major Crisis Overcome
After the pandemic, the world entered a profound “Loneliness Crisis.” By 2027, it had reached its peak, marked by widespread anxiety, digital isolation, and emotional burnout. In response, a global shift emerged: governments, technology leaders, and civil society turned away from systems of emotional surveillance and embraced empathetic design instead. Out of this collective awakening, RaízMental was born—proving that the future of mental health lies not in control, but in compassion and care.
