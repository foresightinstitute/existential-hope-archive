🌍 World Summary
Vision Statement
Strong AI is our chance to end human aging and diseases and open the road to even more robust and durable human bodies and minds.

🔬 Technology
Molecular Nanotechnology
AI-designed medical nanobots live inside human bodies and completely solve the aging and diseases.

Life in 2035: People maintain young, healthy bodies at any age. However, not everyone has access to full treatment, creating visible inequality between those who age and those who don’t.

What kind of AI exists in your world?
Strong tool AI specialized in molecular nanotechnology design exists. It designs but doesn’t decide – humans verify and approve all designs.

Usage: Scientists collaborate with AI in design studios, sketching concepts that AI develops into molecular configurations.

Who builds it: Initially, independent scientists crowdfunded AI development. Later, biotech corporations joined the effort. The combination of open research and corporate resources accelerated progress.

New or Reformed Institution
International Council of Life Extension makes life extension technology accessible worldwide.

Purpose:

Lobbies governments for subsidies and social programs covering nanobot treatments
Assists companies with scaling manufacturing to reduce costs
Coordinates technology standards for mass production
Advocates for laws requiring companies to allocate treatments for underserved populations
Promotes open-source research to prevent monopolization
 

Transformed Sector
Healthcare
Traditional medicine focused on treating diseases. By 2035, it maintains optimal health states. Doctors now program nanobots and customize protocols. Emergency rooms mostly handle accidents and system transitions.

Why it matters: The shift created two parallel medical systems – one for the enhanced, one for traditional patients. Retirement laws changed – people must renounce pension rights to receive anti-aging treatment.

A Major Crisis Overcome
The Nanotech Crisis and Revolution

Challenge: In 2029, several nations began secret military nanotechnology programs, provoking scientists’ protests.

Solution:

Scientists launched crowdfunding campaigns for independent longevity-focused AI and nanotech research
In 2033, nanoweapon incidents nearly triggered world war
International treaties restricted usage of nanoweapons
The independent scientists’ breakthroughs in 2033 launched the longevity revolution
